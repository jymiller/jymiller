The engagement

A commercial bank needed its Snowflake platform taken from early setup to a governed, production-ready foundation. I ran the assessment, did the hands-on provisioning and remediation, and left the team with a sequenced roadmap and the planning system to drive it. The work spanned five areas — platform architecture, provisioning and RBAC, security, data integration, and developer agility — 40 distinct work areas in all.

A governed platform foundation

I provisioned a dedicated Snowflake account on best-practice foundations: a medallion data architecture (Bronze / Silver / Gold plus a staging layer) across development and QA tiers, a shared platform database for cross-cutting objects, and a naming convention applied consistently to every account-level object so code promotes cleanly between environments. 13 databases, 84 schemas, 7 workload-isolated warehouses, and Azure storage integration — all under a two-layer role-based access model that separates functional roles from access roles on the principle of least privilege: 20 custom roles, a full role hierarchy, and over 3,700 privilege grants.

Enterprise identity and security

I integrated enterprise identity end to end — single sign-on and automated user provisioning through Azure AD (SAML2 SSO and SCIM) — so identity is managed centrally rather than per-account. I documented the authentication-policy posture (secondary roles, session controls, break-glass access) and produced an ordered RBAC and ownership remediation runbook to close the gaps the assessment surfaced: ownership consolidation, scoped service accounts, and default-role hygiene. Network-policy tightening and the production identity setup are sequenced into the roadmap.

Source data and a transformation layer

I landed live source data — a CRM data share across the development and QA environments — and stood up the dbt transformation infrastructure: external-access and git integrations, native dbt project objects, and a scheduled-task framework. The platform receives real data and is ready to model it.

Snowflake-native AI in production

The platform runs AI natively inside Snowflake — no external AI infrastructure to secure or operate:

  • An AI agent that answers commercial-lending questions over credit-memo documents (Snowflake Cortex Agents)
  • A Cortex Search service indexing source documents for retrieval-augmented answers
  • Five semantic views — one AI-extended — powering governed, self-service analytics through Cortex Analyst
  • A Streamlit application deployed for document-driven workflows
  • Cortex usage and cost monitoring, so AI spend is observable from day one

An assessment — and a system the team runs themselves

I assessed the platform across 40 work areas and left the client more than a report. The deliverable is a planning system: a discovery taxonomy and crosswalk (YAML-driven, with build scripts that regenerate the reports), an interactive fix-planner for working the backlog, a snapshot toolchain that captures live account state as evidence, a 10-phase production-standup playbook, and an independent architecture review. Every work area is classified — 12 delivered in-engagement, 10 addressed in the agreed plan, 18 scoped for follow-on — each with evidence or a plan reference. The team operates the system going forward.
